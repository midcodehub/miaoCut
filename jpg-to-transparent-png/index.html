Tips for the Cleanest Transparent PNG

  • For signatures, scan or photograph on plain white paper with even light — then use Fine mode so thin ink strokes are preserved.
  • For logos pulled from print samples or screenshots, use Fast mode — it produces sharper, harder edges, which is what logos want.
  • For portraits and pet photos, switch to Fine mode. Alpha matting handles hair, fur, and soft edges much better than hard cutouts.
  • Higher subject-to-background contrast always helps, but is not required — MiaoCut handles cluttered backgrounds, just slower edges than studio shots.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why can't I just rename a JPG file to PNG to make it transparent?

JPG files store no alpha channel — every pixel is fully opaque. Renaming the extension does not add transparency. To get a real transparent PNG you must remove the background pixels and re-encode as PNG with alpha, which is what MiaoCut does in one step.

Does it work on JPG photos with a busy or non-solid background?

Yes. MiaoCut uses an AI background removal model (BiRefNet) instead of simple color-matching, so it handles textured walls, outdoor scenes, and other complex backgrounds where Magic Wand or eraser tools would fail.

Can I use this for signatures, logos, and profile pictures?

Yes — these are the most common use cases. Scan a signature on paper, snap a logo from a print sample, or upload a portrait photo, and download a transparent PNG you can drop into documents, slides, websites, or designs.

Is there a file size or resolution limit?

Yes. Single uploads are capped at 12 MB and around 25 megapixels to keep the free service stable. Most phone photos and design files fit comfortably under those limits.

Is it really free? Any watermark or signup?

Free with no signup, no credit card, and no watermark on the output. Fair-use rate limits apply (a few cutouts per minute per IP) to keep the service available for everyone.
